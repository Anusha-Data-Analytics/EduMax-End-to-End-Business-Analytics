# Revenue Analysis Report

## EduMax – End-to-End Business Analytics

### Business Domain

EdTech Analytics

### Objective

Investigate whether EduMax's revenue performance aligns with learner enrollment growth and identify opportunities to support revenue-related business decisions through data analysis.

---

# Business Background

EduMax leadership observed that the platform's growing learner base did not appear to translate into proportional revenue growth. Before implementing business changes, stakeholders wanted to determine whether this represented a genuine business issue or simply a normal pattern in learner purchasing behavior.

To address this concern, a structured revenue analysis was conducted using enrollment and payment data.

---

# Business Concern

**Primary Question**

> Why is revenue not growing proportionally with enrollments?

To answer this question, the analysis was divided into four business investigations:

1. Revenue Funnel Analysis
2. Revenue Concentration Analysis
3. Revenue Driver Analysis
4. Revenue Performance Dashboard

---

# Analysis Summary

## 1. Revenue Funnel Analysis

### Business Questions

* Where do registered users leave the revenue funnel?
* Is there revenue leakage between payment and enrollment?

### Key Findings

* **21.92%** of registered users did not enroll in any course.
* **78.08%** of registered users successfully enrolled.
* No users attempted payment without successfully enrolling.
* Payment-to-enrollment conversion was effectively **100%**.

### Business Insight

The analysis found no revenue leakage after payment. The primary opportunity for improvement lies in increasing the conversion of registered users into enrolled learners.

---

## 2. Revenue Concentration Analysis

### Business Questions

* Which courses generate the highest revenue?
* Is EduMax overly dependent on a small number of courses?
* Which course categories contribute the most revenue?

### Key Findings

* The top five courses contribute **36.72%** of total revenue.
* The remaining **63.28%** of revenue is distributed across the rest of the course portfolio.
* **Data Science** contributes **34.16%** of total revenue, making it the highest-performing category.

### Business Insight

Although several courses contribute strongly to revenue, the overall portfolio remains reasonably diversified, indicating no excessive dependency on a small number of offerings.

---

## 3. Revenue Driver Analysis

### Business Question

Is revenue primarily driven by enrollment volume, course pricing, or a combination of both?

### Analysis Approach

Courses were segmented into four groups:

* Price Driven
* Enrollment Driven
* Both Price and Enrollment Driven
* Low Price and Low Enrollment

### Key Findings

* Revenue is influenced by multiple business factors rather than a single driver.
* High learner demand contributes significantly to revenue generation in some categories.
* Premium pricing contributes strongly to revenue generation in other categories.
* Revenue is supported by a balanced combination of enrollment volume and course pricing.

### Business Insight

The analysis indicates that EduMax's revenue model is diversified across both learner demand and pricing strategy, reducing dependence on any single revenue driver.

---

## 4. Revenue Performance Dashboard

An executive Power BI dashboard was developed to support ongoing monitoring of revenue performance.

The dashboard enables stakeholders to:

* Monitor revenue trends.
* Compare enrollments with revenue performance.
* Track key revenue KPIs.
* Support data-driven business decision-making.

---

# Business Conclusion

The completed analyses indicate that the original stakeholder concern is **not materially impacting EduMax's business performance**.

Revenue generally grows alongside learner enrollments, while periodic revenue fluctuations are primarily associated with premium-priced course enrollments rather than an underlying business issue.

As a result, the initial revenue concern has been considered resolved.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the completed analyses:

* Increase the conversion of registered users into enrolled learners.
* Continue monitoring revenue performance through executive dashboards.
* Maintain a diversified course portfolio to reduce revenue concentration risk.
* Shift analytical focus toward understanding learner behavior after enrollment.
